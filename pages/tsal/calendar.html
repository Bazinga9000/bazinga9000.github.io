How it Works
The Tsalian Calendar is made up of a 711-day year of eighteen months. Each month is named after a color:

Month (1-6) Length (Days) Color Month (7-12) Length (Days) Color Month (12-18) Length (Days) Color

Itsōğus 39 Myogeğus 39 Rocğus 39


In a standard year, known as a Gray Year, all of these months are present. However, the calendar also contains two types of leap year: White Years, which skip the black month Quğus, and Black Years, which skip the white month Rocğus.
These years occur on a 203-year cycle. It's helpful to consider this cycle as being made up of thirteen identical groups of 15 years, followed by a "rump group" of 8 years:
